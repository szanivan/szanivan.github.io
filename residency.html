The Palace Residency

The Palace website

the Event

The Palace is a non-profit arts collective set up for and by artists. It runs events that focus on the interdisciplinary exchange of emerging artists – the main event being an annual artists residency and festival held in a 700-year-old palace in Southern Poland.
The event is an opportunity to step away from the expectations and demands of the professional world, and step into an oasis of time and space where the development process is the end goal. The Palace offers a unique audience of creative minds and engaged peers with which to workshop ideas, receive feedback, develop work and form on-going creative partnerships. We encourage contributors to experiment with new art forms and to explore their craft without fear of failure or judgement.

my Role

    Pre-event: Building up to the residency, one of my main tasks wa material sourcing, scouting for the best options to buy the necessary materials. Gathering the material list was a complex process of communicating with the various people coming to the Palace with various wishes from various disciplines. These disciplines have been divided among the team, I was mainly involved with groups needing audio-visual support. These were mainly the light designers and the recording booth, where the music workshops were held. I was working closely together with the light designers, and nbased on a budget built up an inventory of LED bulbs, LED strips, strahler lights, portable lights, zubehör, such as cables, batteries, charging possibilities. Another task that I was involved in is setting up the infrastructure on the location. This involved providing internet for 200 people in a remote area. Gearing up the organisation's team with walkie-talkies, printing possibilities. Thinking about the money flow at the residency and devising and acquiring a token system, bank card payment options. Gathering all the resources for wristbands. Making Palace merchandise to be sold on the residency (tote bags, condoms). During event: On the residency firstly manning the welcome booth, checking in, ticketing, signing a consent form. Also managing key contributors transport and check in. General technical and logistical support for the light designers. Organising and manning the Site Awareness team, who regularly check the location and facilities, smooth running of ongoing events and well-being and safety of all festival participants. Being on the Info point, managing token exchange, selling merchandise and other commodities, daily counting of the money and transferring it to the financial team, be available for info, lost&found, new project announcements, emergencies.

the Team

The Palace team consists of international volunteers who take up on different levels of responsibility. The number reaches around 40 people.
